🌱 WHY WE EXIST

Modern engineering is outgrowing our ability to manage it.

Cloud infrastructure spans thousands of resources, many running untracked and unmonitored. Career growth requires developers to scan hundreds of job postings, manage dozens of customized resumes, and prepare for interviews manually. The systems we interact with every day are growing increasingly complex.

Developers now spend more time operating tools, configuring scripts, and managing overhead than solving core problems. We believe this complexity is a tax on engineering creativity.

SiliconRoots exists to build elegant, open-source software that eliminates this noise. We design tools that prioritize localized data execution, simple architectures, and immediate usability—so you can focus on building.

Every tool we create is driven by a single goal: make complex work feel simple.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Straight answers about Silicon Roots—who we are, why we exist, and where we're headed.

What is Silicon Roots?

Silicon Roots is an engineering organization that builds practical, open-source infrastructure software for the teams who run modern systems—DevOps, SRE, platform, and cloud engineers. We ship our work in the open and expect our portfolio of projects to grow over time.

Why was Silicon Roots created?

Cloud environments grow faster than our ability to understand them. Context gets scattered across consoles, accounts, and tribal knowledge. We started Silicon Roots to build the kind of software that makes infrastructure easier to understand than it is to create—and to do it in the open.

What kind of software does Silicon Roots build?

Practical infrastructure tooling: software that helps engineering teams discover, understand, govern, and operate the systems they're responsible for. We start from real operational problems and ship tools that solve them, rather than building features for their own sake.

Why open source?

Tools this close to your infrastructure should be inspectable and trustworthy. We build in the open under permissive licenses (Apache-2.0) so you can read exactly how our software works—not take our word for it. Open collaboration also makes the engineering better.

How can I contribute?

Everything happens on GitHub. Open an issue, start a discussion, improve the documentation, or send a pull request. Fixes, ideas, and entirely new capabilities are all welcome, and contributions are reviewed in the open.

How can I follow development?

Watch and star our repositories on GitHub, follow issues and discussions, and read the docs. Because we develop in public, the code and conversations are the most accurate picture of where our engineering efforts are headed.

How can I get involved?

Pick what fits you: browse the code on GitHub, open an issue, join a discussion, improve documentation, or send a pull request. You can also reach out by email if you want to collaborate on a new idea or propose a project. There is no gatekeeping—if you care about infrastructure software, there is a place for you here.

What is StackPilot?

StackPilot is an open-source product in our ecosystem: an agentless cloud intelligence platform for AWS. It discovers resources across accounts and regions, builds a unified inventory, maps topology, and surfaces governance, security, and operational insights through a single console.

How can I try StackPilot?

It is open source and self-hostable. Head to the repository on GitHub to read the documentation, run it locally or in your own Kubernetes cluster, and connect an AWS account with a read-only role.

What is HirePilot?

HirePilot is our local-first AI career operating system. It parses your resume, builds a Career Knowledge Graph, discovers jobs in parallel across 13+ sources via Celery background workers, scores match fit, and prepares evidence-backed application packages—all stored on your local hardware.

How can I try HirePilot?

HirePilot is completely open source under the MIT License. You can clone the repository from GitHub, run it locally via Docker Compose, and upload your resume to get started in minutes. It runs fully local models with Ollama, keeping your data private.
