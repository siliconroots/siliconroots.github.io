🌱 WHO WE ARE

A small engineering organization focused on infrastructure software.

We're builders—not a product company. Silicon Roots exists because the teams who run modern systems deserve software that is practical, honest, and inspectable. We write code in public, learn from real operational problems, and ship tools that help people understand what they operate.

Why we build

Infrastructure is outgrowing our ability to understand it. Cloud environments spread across accounts, regions, and teams—and the context needed to operate them safely gets lost along the way. We build software to close that gap.

What motivates us is simple: operational clarity over accidental complexity, and open source over black boxes. Tools this close to your infrastructure should be readable and trustworthy—not something you have to take on faith.

🚀 FLAGSHIP PROJECT

HirePilot

Your Personal AI Career Operating System

An AI-powered local-first career operating system that understands your resume, continuously discovers matching opportunities, tailors resumes, prepares interviews and helps professionals land better jobs.

View on GitHub Documentation

What It Does

📄

Resume Intelligence

Automatically parses PDF, DOCX, TXT, and Markdown files to extract skills, experience, and target roles.

🕸️

Career Knowledge Graph

Connects skills, experiences, and evidence nodes to map your career profile in PostgreSQL.

🔍

AI Job Discovery

Celery workers scrape 13+ sources in parallel based on your profile, preferences, and locations.

🎯

Resume Tailoring

Generates tailored resume variants and cover letters grounded strictly in real evidence from your profile.

💬

Interview Preparation

Generates custom question packs, talking points, and answers per shortlisted role to get you prepared.

🏢

Company Intelligence

Researches company details, tech stacks, funding, and culture to give you deep context before interviewing.

💰

Salary Intelligence

Analyzes market salary benchmarks and sets strategies for negotiation based on local compensation rates.

🎛️

Career Command Center

A morning briefing dashboard showing excellent matches, prioritized actions, and daily goals.

🛡️

Privacy First

Runs entirely on your hardware with local models. No remote analytics, telemetry, or account registrations.

Technical Specifications

Architectural constraints and platform attributes.

Data Ingest Policy Local-Only / Privacy-First (No external storage, all data is retained on the user's hardware)
Security Gate Enforced Manual Approval (Submission disabled by default; `APPLICATION_SUBMISSION_ENABLED=false`)
Execution Stack FastAPI (Backend) / Next.js (Frontend) / Celery & Redis (Workers) / PostgreSQL (Database)
Licensing Model Permissive MIT License (Open-Source code, local automation, and custom templates)

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Straight answers about Silicon Roots—who we are, why we exist, and where we're headed.

What is Silicon Roots?

Silicon Roots is an engineering organization that builds practical, open-source infrastructure software for the teams who run modern systems—DevOps, SRE, platform, and cloud engineers. We ship our work in the open and expect our portfolio of projects to grow over time.

Why was Silicon Roots created?

Cloud environments grow faster than our ability to understand them. Context gets scattered across consoles, accounts, and tribal knowledge. We started Silicon Roots to build the kind of software that makes infrastructure easier to understand than it is to create—and to do it in the open.

What kind of software does Silicon Roots build?

Practical infrastructure tooling: software that helps engineering teams discover, understand, govern, and operate the systems they're responsible for. We start from real operational problems and ship tools that solve them, rather than building features for their own sake.

Why open source?

Tools this close to your infrastructure should be inspectable and trustworthy. We build in the open under permissive licenses (Apache-2.0) so you can read exactly how our software works—not take our word for it. Open collaboration also makes the engineering better.

How can I contribute?

Everything happens on GitHub. Open an issue, start a discussion, improve the documentation, or send a pull request. Fixes, ideas, and entirely new capabilities are all welcome, and contributions are reviewed in the open.

How can I follow development?

Watch and star our repositories on GitHub, follow issues and discussions, and read the docs. Because we develop in public, the code and conversations are the most accurate picture of where our engineering efforts are headed.

How can I get involved?

Pick what fits you: browse the code on GitHub, open an issue, join a discussion, improve documentation, or send a pull request. You can also reach out by email if you want to collaborate on a new idea or propose a project. There is no gatekeeping—if you care about infrastructure software, there is a place for you here.

What is StackPilot?

StackPilot is our flagship project: an agentless cloud intelligence platform for AWS. It discovers resources across accounts and regions, builds a unified inventory, maps topology, and surfaces governance, security, and operational insights through a single console.

How can I try StackPilot?

It is open source and self-hostable. Head to the repository on GitHub to read the documentation, run it locally or in your own Kubernetes cluster, and connect an AWS account with a read-only role.

What is HirePilot?

HirePilot is our local-first AI career operating system. It parses your resume, builds a Career Knowledge Graph, discovers jobs in parallel across 13+ sources via Celery background workers, scores match fit, and prepares evidence-backed application packages—all stored on your local hardware.

How can I try HirePilot?

HirePilot is completely open source under the MIT License. You can clone the repository from GitHub, run it locally via Docker Compose, and upload your resume to get started in minutes. It runs fully local models with Ollama, keeping your data private.
