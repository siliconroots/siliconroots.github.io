ENTERPRISE TRUST

Built for Enterprise Platform Security

Why CTOs, CISOs, and Platform Architects trust StackPilot with their cloud estates.

🛡️

Zero-Write Threat Model

StackPilot strictly queries configuration APIs via read-only IAM Cross-Account Roles and Service Principals. We never request, require, or store write credentials. There is zero risk of accidental state modifications or automated outages.

🔍

Continuous Compliance Mapping

Automatically evaluate every cloud asset and Kubernetes configuration against CIS Benchmarks, SOC 2 controls, ISO 27001 mappings, and HIPAA requirements. Instantly pinpoint and trace drift before audit cycles.

🔐

Zero Data-Ingest Policy

We index metadata namespaces only (ARN, resource types, labels, VPC routing tables). StackPilot never reads, caches, or ingests customer database rows, file storage contents, or actual application payloads.

Frequently Asked Questions

Questions about Silicon Roots and StackPilot.

What is Silicon Roots?

Silicon Roots is an engineering organization building open infrastructure software for cloud, platform and infrastructure teams. StackPilot is our flagship project, with further projects—Infrastructure Graph, Policy Engine, Cloud SDK and a Platform Engineering Toolkit—in research and planning.

What is StackPilot?

StackPilot is a cloud intelligence platform that helps engineering teams discover, understand, and govern cloud and Kubernetes infrastructure. By connecting to your accounts, it maps resource dependencies, displays cluster metrics, and builds a unified inventory view of your active systems without console hopping.

Are the future projects available today?

No. Infrastructure Graph, Policy Engine, Cloud SDK and the Platform Engineering Toolkit are projects we are actively researching and designing. They are not shipping products yet—they describe the direction Silicon Roots is heading. StackPilot is the platform you can use today.

Who is StackPilot designed for?

The platform is built for Platform, DevOps, Cloud, and Infrastructure teams managing complex cloud setups. Security teams use it to track compliance drift, FinOps groups use it to audit resource costs, and engineering leaders use it to maintain visibility over system architecture.

Does StackPilot support multiple cloud accounts?

Yes. You can onboard multiple AWS accounts and access them from a unified dashboard. StackPilot provides centralized visibility across accounts and regions while maintaining tenant-aware isolation to ensure secure data separation. Azure and GCP support is on the roadmap.

How are credentials protected?

All credentials are encrypted at rest using AES-GCM encryption in secure storage. We follow a strict philosophy of least privilege, requiring only read-only access schemas, and fully support standard credential rotation processes.

Does StackPilot install agents inside my infrastructure?

No. StackPilot is entirely agentless. It operates by querying AWS and Kubernetes APIs, meaning you do not need to install sidecars, agents, or daemons on your nodes or inside your virtual private clouds.

Is Silicon Roots open source?

Open source is core to how we work. We build in the open, develop transparently, and grow our software with the community that runs it. Follow and contribute to our work on GitHub.

How do I get involved or request a demo?

Engage with us on GitHub through discussions, issues and pull requests, or reach out directly at thesiliconroots@gmail.com to request a StackPilot demo or talk about building with Silicon Roots.
